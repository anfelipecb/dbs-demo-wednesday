Caseworkers at the Agencia Distrital de Empleo are the frontline of Bogota's labor market intermediation system. Each day, they navigate a demanding pipeline: registering jobseekers, building CVs, profiling skills, matching vacancies, and following up on placements — all while managing caseloads that stretch the limits of what one person can accomplish.

The bottleneck is administrative. On average, a single jobseeker requires 4.4 hours of caseworker time before receiving a job referral. Multiply that across 38,844 annual placements and the scale of the challenge becomes clear: the ADE's human resources, no matter how dedicated, are inherently finite.

Enter Nexo

Nexo is a generative AI copilot designed to sit alongside the caseworker — not replace them. The tool automates repetitive, time-intensive tasks: drafting CVs from raw jobseeker data, mapping skills to occupations using patterns learned from top-performing caseworkers, generating ranked vacancy recommendations, and producing follow-up documentation.

Critically, caseworkers retain full discretion at every step. Every AI suggestion can be accepted, edited, or ignored entirely. The tool is designed to encode best practices, not override professional judgment.

Proving It Works

The evaluation follows a randomized controlled trial design. Caseworkers at the ADE will be randomly assigned access to Nexo. The ADE's administrative data — including wages, placement rates, and unemployment spell duration — provides the outcome measurement. A purpose-built survey instrument will capture direct effects on caseworker behavior and workload, as well as adoption rates and barriers.
