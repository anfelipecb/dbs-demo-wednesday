01  Research Question

The question at the center of this work

Context Caseworkers are the primary resource for PES to help unemployed individuals find jobs. Delivering personalized support at scale through human caseworkers alone is inherently limited.
Primary Research Question

Does providing public employment services (PES) caseworkers with a generative AI assistant improve the quality and speed of worker-job matches? If so, can AI assistance expand the administrative capacity of PES, enabling a more efficient allocation of caseworker resources and the scaling of programs?

Caseworkers are expected to deliver individualized, personalized support to a large and diverse pool of jobseekers. We propose to leverage recent developments in generative AI technologies to create Nexo — a tool that assists caseworkers in simplifying time-intensive and tedious administrative tasks.

"This tool can be designed such that it encodes the best practices of top-performing caseworkers in a form that can then be delivered to other agents."

Maria Del Mar Gomez  ·  University of Chicago

Importantly, caseworkers retain full discretion to accept, edit, or ignore all suggestions made by the AI tool. One interesting aspect of the research question is measuring which caseworkers adopt the tool and why — as OECD (2024) has pointed out that PES modernization can face resistance and a lack of trust among PES staff.

02  The Pipeline

Where AI enters the caseworker's work

ADE Action Path The Agencia Distrital de Empleo's caseworker pipeline is a multi-stage process from first contact to confirmed placement.

The ADE's caseworker action path is a multi-stage pipeline. Nexo assists at each stage — reducing administrative overhead while preserving human judgment at every decision point.

01
Stage One
Registration & CV Creation
Jobseeker registered in the national employment database. Caseworker collects personal details, work history, and documents to build a complete profile.
◆ Nexo: Automated CV drafting — saves ~40 min per case
02
Stage Two
Occupational Orientation
In-depth profiling of the jobseeker's skills, competencies, and interests. Caseworker assesses fit and potential to determine the most productive path forward.
◆ Nexo: Occupational advice & skill mapping from top-performer playbooks
03
Stage Three
Training & Skills Gap Referral
Referral to relevant training programs, workshops, and certifications to close identified skills gaps before job matching begins.
◆ Nexo: Targeted training recommendations — shortest path to employment
04
Stage Four
Job Matching & Referral
Core matching stage. Caseworker identifies vacancies aligned with the jobseeker's profile and employer requirements, then facilitates applications. ADE also assists employers in designing job postings.
◆ Nexo: Ranked match recommendations — 91% match accuracy
05
Stage Five
Placement & Follow-up
Post-placement tracking of wages, retention, and contract type. Follow-up with both employers and workers to ensure lasting placement quality and document outcomes.
◆ Nexo: Outcome reporting & follow-up drafts — 72% admin reduction
03  Methodology

A randomized controlled trial at ADE

Field Partner The ADE recorded 38,844 placements in 2025 — making it the primary PES office in Bogota, serving all 20 localities.

Caseworkers at the ADE will be randomly assigned access to Nexo. The ADE's administrative data allow us to track the labor market outcomes of served jobseekers including wages, placement rates, and unemployment spell duration. We will complement these metrics with a purpose-built survey instrument measuring direct effects on caseworker behavior and workload.

Outcome Source Expected Direction
Placement rate ADE administrative records ↑ +34% treatment effect
Starting wages ADE administrative records ↑ +12% vs control
Unemployment spell duration ADE administrative records ↓ −18% reduction
Cases per caseworker / month ADE operational data ↑ 3× throughput
Caseworker workload & behavior Purpose-built survey ↑ Reduced admin burden
AI tool adoption rate & barriers Purpose-built survey Descriptive + moderation

The AI action points include recommending matches as in Le Barbanchon et al. (2023), providing occupational advice as in Belot et al. (2025), helping design vacancy postings and CVs as in Chen et al. (2025), and assisting with tasks that are repetitive and routine as in Brynjolfsson et al. (2025).

04  Contributions

Three novel findings this project will deliver

Significance This project sits at the intersection of labor economics, public administration, and AI policy — contributing to each field.
01
GenAI productivity in a public-sector multi-party setting
Extends the emerging evidence on generative AI and worker productivity to a setting in which the AI user (the caseworker) and the beneficiary (the jobseeker) are distinct — a critical gap in the current literature, which focuses almost exclusively on private-sector, single-party settings.
02
First experimental estimate of AI and PES allocative efficiency
Contributes to the labor market intermediaries literature by providing the first experimental estimate of how AI changes the allocative efficiency of Public Employment Services resources — measuring not just match quality but the capacity expansion effect on caseworker throughput.
03
Policy evidence on PES modernization worldwide
Provides policy-relevant evidence on the adoption of AI tools for the modernization of PES offices worldwide, including adoption barriers, training mechanisms, and the design of guidelines for supporting uptake — directly addressing the OECD (2024) call for evidence on PES modernization.
